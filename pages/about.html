About us

We’re Vivek and Abhilasha.

Same couch, same queue.
Same stubborn taste for stories that do not talk down.

We talk about what we watch the way we talk about everything else that matters.

Coffee, walks, the car, long after the credits.

A scene nags one of us. The other replays it aloud. We argue, laugh, land closer to what the film was asking.

That does not stay on the screen.

A slow-burn betrayal shows up when we read the news. Moral gray at work echoes a procedural. A kitchen joke reminds us of family.

The shelf is personal. Life keeps rhyming with it.

Abhilasha is in every list pick. Every “should we finish this?” Every essay that starts as a late-night text.

Vivek tends spreadsheets and build work.

The voice you read is ours — not a solo act.

Silver Score is that habit on the page: honest scores, essays when something will not let go, lists that feel like a conversation.

No sponsors. No algorithmic wall. No ten-out-of-ten inflation.

Between us: 1,000+ titles. North of 8,500 watch hours on IMDb.

A ledger of what we return to — not what we claim to watch.

The shape of it is us: thrillers, espionage, Nordic noir, moral ambiguity, subtitles, slow burns.

If it trusts the audience, it probably made the cut.

If it needs a cinematic universe to feel important, it probably did not.
