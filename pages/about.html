About this site

A two-person cinema desk — us behind every score, list, and essay.

We’re Vivek and Abhilasha. Between us we’ve logged 1,000+ titles and somewhere north of 8,500 watch hours on IMDb — and we still can’t resist starting a new series at midnight.

Silver Score is our after-hours film & TV desk: honest scores, short essays, and curated lists that move at reading pace. No sponsors, no algorithmic wall, no 10-out-of-10 inflation.

For more about Vivek — career, how he works, and how to reach us — see his About me page (same source as the viveksinha/viveksinha site on GitHub).

This site started with our IMDb ratings and a few charts — a way to see what we actually watch versus what we think we watch. The pattern is clear — thrillers, espionage, Nordic noir, and anything with moral ambiguity and subtitles.

What we publish

  • Stories — our editorial hub: curated lists with a point of view, long-read essays, under-the-radar picks, and release lanes we’re tracking.
  • Upcoming — releases we’re tracking, with filters when you want a tighter view.
  • Browse — the full searchable list of everything we’ve rated, with honest scores.

We lean into tension, slow burns, and stories that trust the audience. If it’s a Scandinavian crime drama or a Cold War spy thriller, it’s probably already on the list. If it’s a superhero franchise, it probably isn’t.

All recommendations are backed by data — roughly 8,500+ hours across 1,000+ titles, years of watching, and zero sponsored content.

How the site looks

The visual direction is Silver Score Noir — a quiet cinema-editorial look. Near-black dark mode for after-hours reading, warm cream light mode for daylight; a silvered Cormorant wordmark over Fraunces headlines, DM Sans body, DM Mono labels.

Five editorial pillars each carry their own accent so you always know which room you’re in: Noir & Thrillers (indigo) is the lead; Beyond English (teal) covers world cinema and TV; Under the Radar (purple) surfaces high scores with low vote counts; Essays & Stories (amber) is the reading desk; Upcoming (green) is the release board. Browse, the full archive, stays neutral on purpose.
